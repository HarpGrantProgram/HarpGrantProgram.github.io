HARP Grant Program

Our Mission

The HARP grant program supports self directed research projects for new students to kickstart their Berkeley careers. It often takes all 4 years at Berkeley to fully navigate university resources, build a network of mentors, and contribute something meaningful to the campus community. We want to accelerate this process by giving students a taste of each of these things in their first year here through a manageable but impactful research project. The grant will provide monetary support for materials, access to campus spaces, and project specific mentors. At the beginning of the semester, individuals or teams of students can apply for the program by submitting a brief proposal for a research project both feasible and community impact oriented. This could range from data collection to engineering a useful device or application to improve student quality of life. Accepted students will draft project plans, collect data or build prototypes, and present and publicize their results at an end of the semester poster session. Freshman can leverage what they build through the HARP program to get the most out of their overall Berkeley experience whether future research opportunities, faculty connections, or new friends and teammates.
